About

I became a therapist after spending nearly a decade in the Army — four years on active duty, including a deployment to Afghanistan, and another five in the Georgia National Guard. My experience shaped how I think about resilience and about what it means to ask for help. It also gave me a deep respect for the people who serve in uniform and in emergency services, and a commitment to making quality mental health care accessible to them.

When I left active duty, I decided to pursue my Masters in Psychology from the University of West Georgia where I completed the necessary coursework to obtain a professional counseling license. I began my clinical career at Ridgeview Institute in Smyrna, GA where I had the opportunity to work in a variety of settings including admissions assessment and inpatient psychiatric group therapy while also practicing supervised outpatient therapy.

After obtaining my full license, I spent 5 years in private practice in Carrollton, Georgia. I also provided group substance use and recovery support group therapy for the Carroll County pretrial diversion program for individuals involved in the criminal justice system. My family and I moved back to Atlanta and I spent two years working with the Emory Addiction Clinic in Decatur before ultimately deciding to return to private practice.

I am committed to providing compassionate, evidence-based care tailored to each individual's needs. I have worked with multiple populations, including veterans, first responders, and individuals navigating substance use challenges. I have work with adolescents struggling with anxiety, depression, substance use, and other mental health concerns.

How I Work

I don't believe in a one-size-fits-all approach. I draw on evidence-based methods tailored to what you're actually dealing with — the Gottman Method for couples, Prolonged Exposure for trauma, and Motivational Interviewing and the Community Reinforcement Approach for clients working through substance use. For families navigating a loved one's substance use, I also use the Community Reinforcement and Family Training(CRAFT) model.

More than any technique, I'm interested in building a relationship where you feel understood and where the work feels real.
